What roles in the Chandler project did Michael Toy, John Anderson, Ted Burgess, Mitchell Kapor, and Lou Montulli hold?

Michael Toy was the project manager. John Anderson was the lead programmer. Mitchell Kapor was the founder and funder of OSAF. Everyone else was a programmer.

What is “Bugzilla?”

A program people enter the bugs they're working on into and the amount of time each should take to fix.

What is OSAF?

Open Source Applications Foundation

What is the projects name?

Chandler

What will the software do?

Manage personal information, such as contact info and schedules.

What is Toy’s keyword for “black hole” bugs?

Scary

What scared Toy so much about Bug 44?

There was no way of knowing how much time it would take to fix the bug.

What did Toy refer to as a “snake?”

Software time because it was "an important problem that we don't have consensus on how to attack."

In the software world, what does “slippage” mean?

Lateness.

Fredrick Brooks was a programming manager for what software project?

IBM system 360

What is Brooks's Law?

Adding man power to a late project makes it later.

Brooks found what % of project time was spent writing code?

17%

Brooks found what % of project time was for testing and fixing bugs?

50%

Brooks observed that the unit of effort named “man-month” only applied under what conditions?

Work that is done without any need for communication between programmers.

What is the one “article of faith” that all “open source” or “free” software advocates share?

Every good work of software starts by scratching a developer's personal itch.

What is the difference between a “good” programmer and a “great” programmer?

Good programmers know what to write. Great ones know what to rewrite (and reuse).

Eric Raymond’s book “The Cathedral and the Bazaar” made a distinction between two important project development ideas, briefly contrast them.

The cathedral approach, being one where a closed group of individuals obsessively devoted to a single piece of software will create something more cohesive. And the bazaar or "promiscuous" approach where code is openly available to be viewed and added to by whoever can. Less cohesive but with out any forseeable boundary.

Has “open source” software project development refuted Brooks’s “mythical man-month” concerns?

Mostly. The allowance of open tinkering and additions doesn't require other people to stop what they're doing to bring someone else on board and get them up to speed. If someone is interested they get on board through their own study, then add what they can. However that's gotta be pretty messy.

What was Andy Hertzfeld’s input when the Chandler project appeared to have stalled?

"The key is getting exciting work going; the rest will take care of itself."
